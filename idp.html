Understanding Internal Developer Platforms

Description: Internal Developer Platforms (IDP) are centralized environments that standardize development, operations, and deployment processes within an organization. They aim to streamline workflows and improve productivity by providing a cohesive pre set of tools and governance. Most company that have implemented DevOps are now having the need to implement some sort of IDP. This IDP can take the shape of overeload CI/CD tools like Jenkins, Github, homegrown and much more. However it is clear that we need something better than lipstick over tranditional tools. For example Jenkins should just be your CI/CD machine and nothing more it should not customize to perform update to operation, monitoring services and it no a service catalog. Github is great for storing source code, running workflows and collaborating at a distributed level but it is not your service catalog. It may hold the source code and even the desire status of your Kubernetes cluster but it is not your service catalog.
